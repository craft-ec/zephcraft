Everything the network runs is a WASM program. The head registry is the open, owner-signed map of heads — it holds program heads, DB roots, and manifests — sharded into a governed, dynamically-resizable number of shards, each a per-shard CraftSQL table with a rotating writer and no committee; a write is validated natively (owner signature + name limit) and upserts one row. A small set of protocol programs the network owns and governs live under Governance; everything else is a user program, listed here by the head registry.

One k-of-n Quorum primitive underlies all authority on the network, in three provenances: governance (the genesis instance — a declared, owner-signed governor set), attestation (user-declared quorums — "set up your own consensus"), and the epoch committee (a computed, rotating quorum that orders network-owned/anchored programs — automated attestation). Separately, verification is open re-execution consistency (any node re-runs a pure function; see settlement verification under economy).

Not a quorum — no fixed set. A producer posts a request; unrelated nodes grab it, re-run the deterministic function, and sign a verdict. It's how the network catches a node claiming a wrong output (e.g. settlement records, app transitions) without trusting anyone.

Two ways to use the grid. Reciprocity (free tier) — serve others and you earn the right to fetch, no money needed, as long as you give back roughly what you take. The token ledger (paid tier) — when you want more than reciprocity allows, you pay into a shared settlement pool, and providers claim from it in proportion to what they served that epoch. Everything below is this node's position in both. Values sitting at zero mean the fleet is doing storage/repair, not metered paid fetches — they fill in once real egress and payments flow.
